# Value Creation Plan v4 — Vantage Managed Services

The VCP is the bridge from entry (~$15M EBITDA, ~$140M EV) to exit (~$26M EBITDA, ~$260M target EV). Each workstream has an owner, a target, a current status (RYG), the **EV at stake**, and the **3–6 month indicators of success** the board watches. The agent ties **Estimated EV Impact**, **What Drove EV Erosion**, and **Tracking on VCP** back to this document.

## Value bridge (entry → target)
| Lever | Entry | Current (Q2-26) | Target (exit) | EV at stake | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Recurring revenue mix | 48% | 56% | 70% | ~$45M | 🟡 behind pace |
| Cyber/MDR attach rate | 22% | 41% | 50% | ~$26M | 🟢 ahead |
| Managed-services gross margin | 38% | 44% | 48% | ~$30M | 🟢 on track |

## Workstream 1 — Recurring-revenue conversion  ·  Owner: CRO (Halvorsen) + COO (Walsh)  ·  🔴/🟡
- **Target:** 48% → 70% recurring mix; NRR 104% → 115%.
- **Current:** mix 56%, NRR 106%. **~$3.2M ARR behind the FY26 conversion plan.**
- **What's driving the gap (EV erosion):** GTM still compensated and run on net-new logos; recurring conversion is hero-led, not a repeatable engine. **Modeled EV drag ~$9M** at the exit multiple if the trajectory holds.
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) recurring mix ≥ 62% by Q4-26; (2) NRR ≥ 110%; (3) comp plan re-weighted to recurring + NRR and live; (4) two reference logos converted project→managed.
- **Risk:** This is **the one red item on the value bridge** and the basis for the CRO performance plan.

## Workstream 2 — Cybersecurity / MDR attach  ·  Owner: CTO (Sørensen)  ·  🟢
- **Target:** attach 22% → 50%.
- **Current:** 41% — **ahead of pace.** The brightest spot in the plan.
- **EV impact:** ~$26M of the bridge rides here. **Concentration risk: it depends heavily on the CTO** (see leadership assessment — high flight risk).
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) attach ≥ 45%; (2) MDR platform 99.9% uptime; (3) **deputy architect hired** to de-single-thread; (4) **CTO retention package closed.**

## Workstream 3 — Delivery margin (industrialization)  ·  Owner: COO (Walsh) + VP Delivery (Iglesias)  ·  🟢
- **Target:** managed-services GM 38% → 48%.
- **Current:** 44% — on track; up ~600bps since the COO took the transformation.
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) offshore NOC/SOC pod **go-live in Q3-26** at target cost-to-serve; (2) automation deflects ≥ 25% of L1 tickets; (3) GM ≥ 46% by Q4-26.
- **Risk:** Migration execution sits with a **newer VP Delivery (low signal)**; COO is stretched covering it.

## Workstream 4 — Buy-and-build (tuck-ins)  ·  Owner: CEO (Reuben) + CFO (Balakrishnan)  ·  🟡
- **Target:** integrate two tuck-ins.
- **Current:** **1 of 2 closed and integrating well.** Tuck-in #2 is **a quarter late** — held up in integration accounting and the slow close, not deal terms.
- **What's driving the gap (EV erosion):** CFO close cycle (12 business days) delayed the #2 close and the associated synergy capture; **modeled EV slippage ~$4M** of timing value.
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) tuck-in #2 closed by Q3-26; (2) close cycle ≤ 7 days; (3) **VP Finance hired** to carry integration accounting.

## Workstream 5 — EBITDA & finance engine  ·  Owner: CFO (Balakrishnan)  ·  🟡
- **Target:** $15M → $26M EBITDA; clean, fast lender reporting.
- **Current:** $19.4M. On pace **but mix-dependent** — overweight in lower-quality project revenue until Workstream 1 turns.
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) close ≤ 7 days; (2) VP Finance hired; (3) covenant headroom report on time every month; (4) lender relationships documented and transferable.
