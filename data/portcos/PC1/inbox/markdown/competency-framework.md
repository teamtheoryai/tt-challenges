# Leadership Competency Framework — Vantage Managed Services

The shared language for assessing executives. Five competency families (mirroring the methodology scorecard), each with a definition and four proficiency anchors. Assessors rate each leader against the **proficiency anchors**; ratings roll into the scorecard dimensions and the 9-box.

## Proficiency scale
| Level | Score band | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Emerging | 1–2 | Inconsistent; needs support; a development priority. |
| Proficient | 3 | Meets the bar for the role today. |
| Strong | 4 | Exceeds; a model for peers. |
| Exceptional | 5 | Best-in-class; sets the standard; ready for bigger scope. |

---

## 1. Leadership & Team
*Builds, retains, and develops a high-performing team; creates a credible successor.*
- **Emerging:** Carries the work personally; team thin or dependent; no successor.
- **Proficient:** Stable team; some delegation; successor identified but not ready.
- **Strong:** Bench developing; delegates real scope; a developing successor.
- **Exceptional:** Deep bench; a ready-now successor; exports talent to the rest of the org.

## 2. Execution & Results
*Delivers against plan / the VCP; industrializes repeatable outcomes.*
- **Emerging:** Misses commitments; hero-driven, not repeatable.
- **Proficient:** Hits most commitments; some process maturity.
- **Strong:** Consistently on/ahead of plan; repeatable engine.
- **Exceptional:** Beats plan and raises the system's ceiling.

## 3. Strategic Fit (future-state)
*Fit for where the business must go — recurring-revenue MSP, security platform, roll-up.*
- **Emerging:** Anchored to the old model (project/break-fix; net-new logos).
- **Proficient:** Understands the future state; executing the shift.
- **Strong:** Drives the future-state agenda; reshapes the function around it.
- **Exceptional:** Defines the future state; a source of thesis upside.

## 4. Financial Acumen
*Owns the numbers — unit economics, margin, NRR, cost-to-serve.*
- **Emerging:** Limited ownership of the relevant P&L/metrics.
- **Proficient:** Owns the metrics; sound day-to-day judgment.
- **Strong:** Uses economics to drive decisions; clean reporting.
- **Exceptional:** Models trade-offs the board relies on.

## 5. Stakeholder Trust
*Board, investor, lender, client, and peer confidence.*
- **Emerging:** Trust not yet established or uneven.
- **Proficient:** Trusted in role.
- **Strong:** Trusted beyond role; a go-to voice.
- **Exceptional:** The relationship the board/lenders rely on.

| COO | Exceptional | Exceptional | Strong | Proficient | Strong |
| CTO | Strong | Strong | Exceptional | Proficient | Strong |
| CRO | Strong | Exceptional | Strong | Strong | Strong |
| VP Delivery | Strong | Strong | Proficient | Proficient | Proficient |
| VP People | Strong | Strong | Proficient | Proficient | Strong |

## How the framework feeds the dashboard
- **9-Box** uses **Execution** (performance axis) × a blend of **Strategic Fit + Leadership & Team** (potential axis).
- **High Potential** = Strong/Exceptional on potential axis *and* runway for materially bigger scope.
- A leader rated **Emerging** on a competency their role requires at **Strong/Exceptional** generates a **Key Risk** and raises **Actionability**.
