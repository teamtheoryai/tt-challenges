# Leadership Re-Assessment — Vantage Managed Services (May 2026)

Independent re-assessment of the executive team ~31 months post-close, scored against the **target scorecards** and the **competency framework**. For each leader: current-state score, future-state projection (vs. role target), 9-box, hi-po call, risk of loss, succession, key risks, and recommended action. Confidence (**signal**) reflects the volume and consistency of evidence.

> Scoring scale 1–5. RYG: 1–2 red, 3 yellow, 4–5 green. "Future state" is the projected score against the role's **Target** as the business scales over the next 12–18 months.

---

## Marcus Reuben — CEO
- **Current state: 4.1 (green).** Baseline at close was 4.4. The platform-CEO thesis is playing out: he is the engine behind both tuck-in pipelines and carries the board and lenders.
- **Future state: 3.9 (green)** vs. target 4.3. Slight gap: still over-indexes on net-new logos vs. same-client NRR — the one habit flagged at entry that persists.
- **9-box: 1A (High performance / High potential).** **Hi-po: Yes.**
- **Risk of loss: Low (green).** Strongly aligned, equity-motivated; refresh under discussion.
- **Succession — short-term (<6mo):** COO (Walsh), **ready-now**. **Long-term:** COO (Walsh), developing into the full platform-CEO scope.
- **Succession readiness: High (green).**
- **Key risks:** Over-weights new logos vs. NRR; carries M&A largely himself.
- **Signal: High (0.86)** — abundant, consistent evidence.
- **Additional comments:** "Board confidence is high. He's grown into the platform-CEO role." (assessor)
- **Actionability: Medium** — *Protect and incentivize; add an NRR gate to balance the new-logo bias; formalize Walsh as successor.*

> **Evidence —** "Marcus has hit every board gate and closed the first tuck-in cleanly; the second is only late because of accounting, not him." — Board Chair, board review, p.6 §CEO.

## Priya Balakrishnan — CFO
- **Current state: 3.6 (yellow).** Baseline 3.8. Financial acumen remains a 5; but the **close cycle is slow (12 business days)** and has twice delayed lender reporting, and she **still has not built a finance bench**.
- **Future state: 3.0 (yellow)** vs. target 4.2. The gap is structural: the role's heavily-weighted "build the bench" dimension is **Emerging** while the target is **Strong**. Until she hires under herself, she cannot reach thesis-ready.
- **9-box: 2B (Medium performance / Medium potential).** **Hi-po: No** (not for lack of ability — for lack of delegation runway).
- **Risk of loss: Medium (yellow).** She has been approached twice; the CEO reads it as "a flag, not a threat," the assessor rates it higher.
- **Succession — short-term:** **None identified (gap).** **Long-term:** external search required (gap). A controller is the only finance hire beneath her.
- **Succession readiness: Low (red).** "There is no number two in finance today. Full stop."
- **Key risks:** **Single point of failure** on lender + integration-accounting relationships; no bench; slow close.
- **Signal: Medium (0.71)** — clear on the bench/close facts; the retention read is contested (see deviation).
- **Additional comments:** "Technically excellent and holds every banking relationship personally. The risk isn't her competence — it's her irreplaceability."
- **Actionability: High** — *Open a VP Finance search immediately; map and transfer lender relationships; put a retention grant in place.*
- **Deviation (stakeholder contradiction):** CEO frames retention risk as low; assessor rates it medium-high.

> **Evidence —** "Our close took 12 days again this quarter and the lender noticed." — CFO, board deck p.11 §Finance. // "There is no number two in finance today. Full stop." — Assessor, this report §Bench.
