# Organizational Due Diligence — Project Summit (Vantage Managed Services)

**Confidential — prepared for the DAW CAPITAL Investment Committee ahead of close.**

## 1. Purpose & method

This report assesses the leadership team of Vantage Managed Services ("Vantage," "the Company") as part of confirmatory diligence. It establishes the **talent baseline at deal close** against which post-close performance will be measured. Each executive was assessed against the DAW CAPITAL target scorecard for their role (1–5 scale; see `target-scorecards`), through a 90-minute structured interview, reference calls, and review of the prior owner's performance records.

The scores in §4 become the **Interview Performance baseline** (`basis: org-DD-at-close`) in the talent review. They are the anchor for every later "current vs. baseline" comparison.

## 2. Company & thesis context

Vantage is a Denver-based MSP (~$92M revenue, ~$15M EBITDA, ~640 FTE) serving mid-market clients with managed IT, cloud migration/FinOps, and cybersecurity/MDR. DAW's thesis is a **recurring-revenue and buy-and-build play**: convert project/break-fix revenue to managed-services MRR (48% → 70% recurring), lift cybersecurity attach (22% → 50%), integrate two tuck-ins, and expand EBITDA to ~$26M.

The thesis is **people-dependent**: it requires a CEO who can run M&A, a finance function that can absorb integrations, an operating leader who can industrialize delivery, and a technical leader who can build a security platform. Talent risk is therefore a primary diligence question, not a secondary one.

## 4. Executive assessments & baseline scores

Scale: 1 = significant concern · 3 = meets · 5 = exceptional. RYG: 1–2 red, 3 yellow, 4–5 green.

### 4.1 Marcus Reuben — CEO
- **Tenure at close:** 9 months (joined Jan 2023 under prior owner; retained).
- **Baseline score: 4.4 (green).** Strong on strategic fit and stakeholder trust; proven roll-up operator from a prior MSP.
- **Direct cost:** $940,000 total target comp (base $480k + bonus + LTIP).
- **Reference signal:** "He's the rare MSP CEO who can both sell and integrate." (former board member).
- **Succession at close:** COO (Walsh) is the natural successor; ready in 12–18 months.
- **Risk at entry:** Over-indexes on net-new logos vs. same-client expansion — a habit to watch.

### 4.2 Priya Balakrishnan — CFO
- **Tenure at close:** 0 months (joins at close from a PE-backed software CFO seat).
- **Baseline score: 3.8 (green).** Strong financial acumen; question mark on whether she builds a team vs. holds the relationships herself.
- **Direct cost:** $580,000 total target comp.
- **Reference signal:** "Brilliant technically. Be sure she hires under herself early." (prior CEO reference).
- **Succession at close:** **No internal successor.** Single deepest finance hire is a controller.
- **Risk at entry:** Will personally own lender and integration-accounting relationships — **key-person risk flagged at entry**.

### 4.3 Devin Walsh — COO
- **Tenure at close:** 14 months (founder-era, retained).
- **Baseline score: 4.0 (green).** The operating engine. Strong execution and team-building; the assessor's pick for CEO succession.
- **Direct cost:** $560,000 total target comp.
- **Reference signal:** "If anything happened to Marcus, Devin runs the company on day one." (assessor).
- **Succession at close:** VP Delivery (to be hired) is the intended developing successor.
- **Risk at entry:** Will be stretched across both run-the-business ops and the delivery-industrialization transformation.

### 4.4 Anika Sørensen — CTO
- **Tenure at close:** 3 months (joined mid-2023).
- **Baseline score: 3.9 (green).** Excellent platform and security architect; the technical spine of the cyber-attach thesis. Newer to people-leadership at scale.
- **Direct cost:** $510,000 total target comp.
- **Reference signal:** "Top-decile security architect — and the market knows it." (reference). **Recruitment/flight risk flagged at entry** given the hot cyber market.
- **Succession at close:** No internal successor; a principal architect is a long-term candidate.
- **Risk at entry:** Highly recruitable; retention economics must keep pace.

### 4.5 Brad Halvorsen — CRO
- **Tenure at close:** 0 months (joins at close).
- **Baseline score: 3.5 (yellow).** Polished, credible in the room. Diligence could **not fully corroborate** the "turnaround of a $40M services book" claim on his record — referenced as "contributed to" by one source rather than "led."
- **Direct cost:** $440,000 total target comp (heavy variable component).
- **Reference signal:** Mixed — one strong, one lukewarm ("good closer, lighter on building a repeatable engine").
- **Succession at close:** No internal successor.
- **Risk at entry:** **Public-record discrepancy** logged; recurring-revenue conversion is a different motion than net-new logos and unproven for him.

### 4.6 Renee Castellano — VP People
- **Tenure at close:** 2 months.
- **Baseline score: 3.9 (green).** Pragmatic, well-liked, data-driven on attrition.
- **Direct cost:** $280,000 total target comp.
- **Succession at close:** An HR director is a developing successor.
- **Risk at entry:** Low. Function is small and underinvested.

### 4.7 VP Delivery — (Day-1 vacancy)
- Identified as the **single most important post-close hire.** Runs the NOC/SOC and is the linchpin for the delivery-margin workstream. Hired April 2025 (Tomás Iglesias) — see later docs.

## 6. Key talent risks at entry (for the IC)

1. **CFO key-person risk** — lender and integration-accounting relationships concentrate in one person with no bench.
2. **CRO capability/record risk** — unproven on recurring conversion; one unverifiable claim on record.
3. **CTO flight risk** — top-decile, highly recruited, thin retention package inherited.
4. **Delivery leadership gap** — VP Delivery seat empty at a moment when delivery margin is central to the thesis.

## 7. Recommendation

Proceed to close. Talent is **net a thesis enabler**, with three named risks to manage from Day 1: hire a VP Delivery within two quarters, put a real retention package behind the CTO, and gate the CRO on recurring-revenue conversion (not just logos). Re-assess the full team at the 18-month mark.
