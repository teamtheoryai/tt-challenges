# Organizational Due Diligence — Project Ridge (Ridgeline Freight & Logistics)

**Confidential — prepared for the DAW CAPITAL Investment Committee ahead of close.**

## 1. Purpose & method

Assesses the leadership of Ridgeline Freight & Logistics ("Ridgeline," "the Company") and sets the **talent baseline at deal close**. Each executive was scored against the DAW target scorecard for their role (1–5 scale), via structured interview, reference calls, and review of operating records. Scores in §4 become the **Interview Performance baseline** (`basis: org-DD-at-close`).

## 2. Company & thesis context

Ridgeline is a Salt Lake City–based regional LTL carrier + 3PL (~$160M revenue, ~$26M EBITDA, ~900 employees incl. ~450 drivers, ~12 terminals) across the Mountain West and Midwest. DAW's thesis is a **density + technology + margin play**: optimize lanes and the terminal network, roll out a modern TMS and telematics, complete one tuck-in terminal acquisition, cut fuel + maintenance cost per mile, improve safety/CSA scores, and expand EBITDA to ~$34M.

Unlike a turnaround, this is a **"good-to-great" thesis on a well-run asset.** The dominant talent question is **CEO succession**: founder Hank Dreyer has run Ridgeline for 31 years and intends to retire within the hold period. The thesis depends on an **orderly handoff** to a credible successor.

## 4. Executive assessments & baseline scores

Scale: 1 = significant concern · 3 = meets · 5 = exceptional. RYG: 1–2 red, 3 yellow, 4–5 green.

### 4.1 Hank Dreyer — CEO (founder)
- **Tenure at close:** 31 years.
- **Baseline score: 4.3 (green).** Deep industry mastery, exceptional customer and community relationships, conservative balance-sheet discipline. **Explicitly plans to retire in ~2–3 years.**
- **Direct cost:** $880,000 total target comp.
- **Reference signal:** "Hank *is* Ridgeline to a lot of shippers. The whole game is handing that off well." (reference).
- **Succession at close:** COO (Whitman), **ready-soon** — the central succession plan.
- **Risk at entry:** **Planned-departure / succession-execution risk** is the defining issue, not performance.

### 4.2 Walter Ngiraingas — CFO
- **Tenure at close:** 0 months (joins at close from a logistics-sector CFO seat).
- **Baseline score: 3.9 (green).** Strong, steady finance leader; clean reporting; good capital-planning discipline for fleet capex.
- **Direct cost:** $520,000.
- **Succession at close:** A controller, developing.
- **Risk at entry:** Low. New to the team but a known quantity.

### 4.3 Tom Whitman — COO
- **Tenure at close:** 28 months (recruited two years before the sale to professionalize ops).
- **Baseline score: 4.1 (green).** The operating engine and the **designated CEO successor.** Ran network/terminal operations at a larger carrier.
- **Direct cost:** $560,000.
- **Reference signal:** "Tom can run this company today. The only question is timing." (board reference).
- **Succession at close:** A VP of Operations, developing.
- **Risk at entry:** Low — the key is a clear, dated path to CEO so he stays for it.

### 4.4 Lena Osei — VP Sales (CRO)
- **Tenure at close:** 0 months (joins at close).
- **Baseline score: 3.8 (green).** Strong commercial leader; brings 3PL/brokerage growth experience to complement the asset-based book.
- **Direct cost:** $400,000 (variable-weighted).
- **Risk at entry:** Low; growing the 3PL mix is the upside she owns.

### 4.5 Sanjay Mehta — VP Fleet & Maintenance
- **Tenure at close:** 4 months.
- **Baseline score: 3.7 (green).** Owns tractors/trailers, maintenance, and the **TMS + telematics** rollout; strong on cost-per-mile.
- **Direct cost:** $340,000.
- **Risk at entry:** Aging-fleet capex timing; technology adoption across drivers.

### 4.6 Carla Jennings — VP Safety & Compliance
- **Tenure at close:** 0 months (joins at close).
- **Baseline score: 3.6 (green).** Owns DOT/CSA scores, insurance posture, and driver-safety programs — a value-critical seat for an asset-based carrier.
- **Direct cost:** $290,000.
- **Risk at entry:** Low; CSA improvement is a direct insurance-cost lever.

### 4.7 Roy Tibbets — VP People
- **Tenure at close:** 0 months (joins at close).
- **Baseline score: 3.5 (yellow).** Solid HR leader; the central challenge is the perennial one — **driver retention** (industry turnover ~70%).
- **Direct cost:** $260,000.
- **Risk at entry:** Driver turnover is the recurring operating risk.

## 6. Key talent risks at entry (for the IC)

1. **CEO-succession execution** — the founder will retire in the hold; the handoff to the COO is the defining talent task.
2. **Driver retention** — the structural operating risk for any asset-based carrier.
3. **Aging-fleet capex** — a financial/operational risk owned by the VP Fleet, not a leadership-quality risk.

## 7. Recommendation

Proceed to close. **Talent is a clear thesis enabler** — an experienced, stable team with a ready successor. Day-1 priorities: agree a **dated CEO-succession plan** (Dreyer → Whitman), lock the COO with a clear path and equity, and set driver-retention targets. Re-assess at 18 months with succession-readiness as the headline metric.
