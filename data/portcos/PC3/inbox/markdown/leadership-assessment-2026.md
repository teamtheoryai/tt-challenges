# Leadership Re-Assessment — Ridgeline Freight & Logistics (May 2026)

Independent re-assessment ~29 months post-close, scored against the **target scorecards** and **competency framework**. **Overall verdict: a mature, well-run team tracking the plan; the headline watch item is the orderly CEO succession.** For each leader: current-state score, future-state projection (vs. role target), 9-box, hi-po call, risk of loss, succession, key risks, recommended action, and confidence (**signal**).

> Scale 1–5. RYG: 1–2 red, 3 yellow, 4–5 green. For the **CEO**, future state intentionally reflects the retirement horizon (a planned transition, not a miss).

---

## Hank Dreyer — CEO (founder)
- **Current state: 4.2 (green).** Baseline 4.3 — essentially flat. Still the best customer and community relationships in the region; the business is humming.
- **Future state: 3.6 (yellow)** vs. target 4.3 — **by design.** He intends to retire in **~18 months**; the future-state CEO is the COO. The yellow reflects **transition risk, not declining performance.**
- **9-box: 1B (High performance / transition-horizon potential).** **Hi-po: n/a** (incumbent founder).
- **Risk of loss: Planned (yellow).** Not a flight risk — a **scheduled departure** that must be managed.
- **Succession — short-term (<6mo):** COO (Whitman), **ready-now.** **Long-term:** COO (Whitman), ready-now.
- **Succession readiness: High (green)** — the strongest succession answer in the portfolio.
- **Key risks:** **Customer relationships concentrated in him**; the handoff of those relationships is the real work of the transition.
- **Signal: High (0.9).**
- **Additional comments:** "Hank is excellent and leaving on schedule. The entire job is transferring his shipper relationships to Tom before he goes."
- **Actionability: Medium** — *Execute a dated 18-month succession plan; begin transitioning key accounts to Whitman now.*

> **Evidence —** "Hank is Ridgeline to a lot of shippers; the whole game is handing that off well." — reference, org DD. // "I want a clean, dated handoff to Tom — no surprises for customers or drivers." — CEO, board deck p.6 §Succession.
