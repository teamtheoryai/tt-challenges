# Leadership Competency Framework — Ridgeline Freight & Logistics

The shared assessment language. Five competency families (mirroring the methodology scorecard), each with four proficiency anchors written for an **asset-based carrier + 3PL.** Ratings roll into the scorecard dimensions and the 9-box.

## Proficiency scale
| Level | Score band | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Emerging | 1–2 | Inconsistent; a development priority. |
| Proficient | 3 | Meets the bar for the role today. |
| Strong | 4 | Exceeds; a model for peers. |
| Exceptional | 5 | Best-in-class; ready for bigger scope. |

---

## 1. Leadership & Team
*Builds, retains, develops the team; for the COO, builds the bench he'll need as CEO.*
- **Emerging:** High turnover; carries work personally; no successor.
- **Proficient:** Stable team; successor identified but not ready.
- **Strong:** Bench developing; retention above the freight-industry norm; delegates real scope.
- **Exceptional:** Deep bench; a ready-now successor; sets the standard.

## 2. Execution & Results
*Delivers against plan / the VCP — density, cost-per-mile, safety, service.*
- **Emerging:** Misses commitments; reactive.
- **Proficient:** Hits most commitments; some process maturity.
- **Strong:** Consistently on/ahead of plan; repeatable across terminals.
- **Exceptional:** Beats plan and raises the network's ceiling.

## 3. Strategic Fit (future-state)
*Fit for a technology-enabled, density-optimized regional carrier + 3PL.*
- **Emerging:** Anchored to legacy / manual operating habits.
- **Proficient:** Understands the future state; executing the shift.
- **Strong:** Drives the future-state agenda across the network.
- **Exceptional:** Defines the model; a source of thesis upside.
- *Note:* for the **incumbent CEO**, "future-state fit" is assessed against the retirement horizon — a declining requirement by design.

## 4. Financial Acumen
*Owns the numbers — cost-per-mile, fleet capex, freight unit economics, capital.*
- **Emerging:** Limited ownership of the relevant economics.
- **Proficient:** Owns the metrics; sound judgment.
- **Strong:** Uses economics to drive decisions; clean reporting.
- **Exceptional:** Models trade-offs the board relies on.

## 5. Stakeholder Trust
*Board, shipper/customer, lender, driver, and regulator confidence.*
- **Emerging:** Trust uneven.
- **Proficient:** Trusted in role.
- **Strong:** Trusted beyond role; a go-to voice.
- **Exceptional:** The relationship the customers / board rely on (the founder-CEO's strength).

## Role requirement matrix (minimum proficiency to be "thesis-ready")

| Role | Lead & Team | Execution | Strategic Fit | Financial | Stakeholder |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CEO (incumbent) | Exceptional | Strong | Strong* | Strong | Exceptional |
| COO (successor) | Exceptional | Exceptional | Exceptional | Strong | Exceptional |
| CFO | Proficient | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong |
| VP Sales | Strong | Strong | Strong | Proficient | Strong |
| VP Fleet | Proficient | Strong | Strong | Strong | Proficient |
| VP Safety | Proficient | Strong | Proficient | Proficient | Strong |
| VP People | Strong | Strong | Proficient | Proficient | Proficient |

*\*CEO Strategic-Fit requirement declines over the retirement horizon as the role transitions to the COO.*

## How the framework feeds the dashboard
- **9-Box** uses **Execution** (performance axis) × a blend of **Strategic Fit + Leadership & Team** (potential axis).
- **High Potential** = Strong/Exceptional on the potential axis *and* runway for bigger scope — at Ridgeline, the **COO** is the standout (CEO-ready).
- The framework's most important output here is **succession readiness**: the COO's Exceptional/Strong profile is what makes the founder-CEO transition low-risk.
