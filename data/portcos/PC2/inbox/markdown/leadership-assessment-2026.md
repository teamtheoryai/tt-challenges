# Leadership Re-Assessment — Cascade Care Group (May 2026)

Independent re-assessment ~34 months post-close, scored against the **target scorecards** and **competency framework**. **Overall verdict: the team is turning a corner but talent risk remains elevated and should stay on the IC's watch list.** Two of the three prior key risks are resolving — the CFO seat is being filled and the commercial engine is recovering — but the **open CEO question and COO retention** keep the overall rating yellow. For each leader: current-state score, future-state projection (vs. role target), 9-box, hi-po call, risk of loss, succession, key risks, recommended action, and confidence (**signal**).

> Scale 1–5. RYG: 1–2 red, 3 yellow, 4–5 green. "Future state" = projected score vs. the role **Target** over the next 12–18 months.

---

## CEO — (seat under review)
- **Status:** The founder-era CEO transition is the **open leadership question.** The board has not resolved whether the next-phase CEO comes from inside (the COO is the credible internal option) or externally. The builder→scaler phase change the thesis requires has not been demonstrated.
- **Future state: 4.2 target** for the next-phase CEO (disciplined multi-site growth + EBITDA-bridge ownership).
- **Risk of loss: n/a** — the more likely path is a **board-initiated succession decision**, not a voluntary departure.
- **Succession — short-term (<6mo):** COO (Nair), **ready-now (as CEO).** **Long-term:** COO (Nair).
- **Succession readiness: High (green)** — the internal bench answer (the COO) is unusually strong, which is what makes a change feasible.
- **Key risks:** Stalled de-novo growth discipline; **risk of losing the COO if the succession question keeps dragging.**
- **Signal: High (0.85).**
- **Additional comments:** "The CEO question is the single open item on this team. The board has the luxury of a strong internal option, but every quarter it stays open is a quarter the COO is fielding approaches."
- **Actionability: High** — *Resolve the CEO question this year; Priya Nair is ready-now as the internal option. Decide before the durability of the rest of the team is tested.*

> **Evidence —** "We opened four clinics this year against a plan of twelve, and two of them are underwater." — Board Chair, board deck p.5 §Growth. // "The board wants the CEO question settled before the September meeting." — Assessor, this report.

## Team-level synthesis (WATCH — improving)
- **Two of the three prior key risks are resolving.** The CFO seat is filled (Naomi Okonkwo — a step-change hire), and the commercial engine is recovering under the CMO (Trevor Hale). Talent risk has moved from **red to yellow** and the team is **trending up.**
- **The remaining priority is retaining the COO (Priya Nair)** — the franchise and the internal CEO option — while the **CEO question stays open.** Losing the COO is the catastrophic scenario.
- **Recommend:** Keep on the IC watch list; resolve the CEO question this year; treat COO retention and the durability of the commercial recovery (Q3 test) as the two things to confirm.
