## p.5 — §Growth (De novo) & Commercial
- **Net-new pipeline +38% QoQ; two of three target logos / partnerships closed** — the first quarter delivering against the commercial plan after two flat quarters.
- De-novo openings still behind plan (4 YTD vs. 12 planned), but the commercial engine that feeds them is recovering.

*Notes (CMO, Trevor Hale):* "Net-new pipeline is up 38% quarter over quarter and we closed two of the three target logos. After two flat quarters this is the first quarter we've delivered against the commercial plan. I want to prove it holds in Q3 before anyone calls it a turnaround."

## p.7 — §Clinical & retention
- **Clinician attrition 28%** (target 15%); **locum/agency spend up ~$4M.**
- Retention function under-resourced; **a funded retention plan is now possible with the CFO in seat.**

*Notes (VP Clinical, Dr. L. Ortiz):* "Clinician attrition hit 28% and our locum spend is up materially. I keep the clinicians who stay; too many are leaving for pay and staffing reasons I couldn't fund alone — now that we have a CFO, we can."

## p.8 — §RCM
- **Net collection rate 92% vs. 98% plan; denials up.** ~$6M annualized cash drag.
- Multi-payor, multi-state complexity outpacing the function; payor analytics now coming online under the CFO.

*Notes (VP RCM, W. Tran):* "Net collections are at 92 against a 98 plan; that's real cash we're leaving on the table, and the denial mix is getting worse, not better. With payor analytics standing up, we finally have a path at it."
