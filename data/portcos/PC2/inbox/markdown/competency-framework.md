# Leadership Competency Framework — Cascade Care Group

The shared assessment language. Five competency families (mirroring the methodology scorecard), each with four proficiency anchors written for a **multi-site care provider**. Ratings roll into the scorecard dimensions and the 9-box.

## Proficiency scale
| Level | Score band | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Emerging | 1–2 | Inconsistent; a development priority. |
| Proficient | 3 | Meets the bar for the role today. |
| Strong | 4 | Exceeds; a model for peers. |
| Exceptional | 5 | Best-in-class; ready for bigger scope. |

---

## 1. Leadership & Team
*Builds, retains, develops the team; for the clinical function, retains clinicians.*
- **Emerging:** High turnover; carries work personally; no successor.
- **Proficient:** Stable team; successor identified but not ready.
- **Strong:** Bench developing; retention above market; delegates real scope.
- **Exceptional:** Deep bench; a ready-now successor; sets the retention standard.

## 2. Execution & Results
*Delivers against plan / the VCP — clinic growth, collections, quality, retention.*
- **Emerging:** Misses commitments; firefighting, not systems.
- **Proficient:** Hits most commitments; some process maturity.
- **Strong:** Consistently on/ahead of plan; repeatable across sites.
- **Exceptional:** Beats plan and raises the system's ceiling.

## 3. Strategic Fit (future-state)
*Fit for a disciplined 80-clinic MSO-backed network — a scaler, not just a builder.*
- **Emerging:** Anchored to single-site / founder-mode habits.
- **Proficient:** Understands the scaled model; executing the shift.
- **Strong:** Drives the future-state agenda across the network.
- **Exceptional:** Defines the model; a source of thesis upside.

## 4. Financial Acumen
*Owns the numbers — clinic-unit economics, payor mix, collections, de-novo capital.*
- **Emerging:** Limited ownership of the relevant economics.
- **Proficient:** Owns the metrics; sound day-to-day judgment.
- **Strong:** Uses economics to drive decisions; clean reporting.
- **Exceptional:** Models trade-offs the board relies on.

## 5. Stakeholder Trust
*Board, clinician, payor, and patient-community confidence.*
- **Emerging:** Trust uneven.
- **Proficient:** Trusted in role.
- **Strong:** Trusted beyond role; a go-to voice.
- **Exceptional:** The relationship the board / clinicians rely on.

## Role requirement matrix (minimum proficiency to be "thesis-ready")

| Role | Lead & Team | Execution | Strategic Fit | Financial | Stakeholder |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CEO | Strong | Exceptional | Strong | Strong | Strong |
| CFO | Strong | Strong | Strong | Exceptional | Strong |
| COO | Exceptional | Exceptional | Exceptional | Strong | Strong |
| CMO (commercial / growth) | Proficient | Strong | Strong | Proficient | Proficient |
| VP RCM | Proficient | Exceptional | Strong | Strong | Proficient |
| VP Clinical | Exceptional | Strong | Proficient | Proficient | Strong |

## How the framework feeds the dashboard
- **9-Box** uses **Execution** (performance axis) × a blend of **Strategic Fit + Leadership & Team** (potential axis).
- **High Potential** = Strong/Exceptional on the potential axis *and* runway for materially bigger scope (at Cascade, the COO is the standout).
- A leader rated **Emerging** on a role-critical competency generates a **Key Risk** and raises **Actionability** — over the hold this has applied to the **CMO** on Execution (pipeline/growth; now recovering) and the **VP RCM** on Execution (collections/denials).
