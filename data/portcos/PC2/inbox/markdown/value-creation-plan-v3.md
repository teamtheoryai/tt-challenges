# Value Creation Plan v3 — Cascade Care Group

The bridge from entry (~$14M EBITDA, ~$130M EV) to exit (~$24M EBITDA, ~$220M target EV). **As of Q2-26 the plan is still off-track on EBITDA (~$11M) but the trajectory is improving:** the CFO seat is filled and the commercial engine is recovering. Each workstream has an owner, target, status (RYG), the **EV at stake**, and the **3–6 month indicators of success.** The agent ties **Estimated EV Impact**, **What Drove EV Erosion**, and **Tracking on VCP** back here.

## Workstream 1 — De-novo growth & commercial pipeline  ·  Owner: CMO (Hale)  ·  🟡 recovering
- **Target:** 48 → 80 clinics, fed by a repeatable de-novo / patient-acquisition pipeline at ~3x coverage.
- **Current:** 52 clinics. After two flat quarters (Q1 pipeline coverage 1.4x vs. 3x target), Q2 was the **first quarter delivering against plan:** **net-new pipeline +38% QoQ** and **two of three target logos / partnerships closed.**
- **What's driving the gap (EV erosion, now easing):** Pipeline was opportunistic for two quarters; modeled EV drag is **narrowing (~$1M net)** as the recovery takes hold. Held cautious pending Q3 durability.
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) net-new pipeline growth sustains into Q3; (2) recovered pipeline converts to opened/booked sites; (3) ≥ 4 disciplined openings booked for H2.
- **Risk:** **Recovery is one quarter old — durability unproven until Q3.**

## Workstream 2 — RCM / collections  ·  Owner: VP RCM (Tran)  ·  🔴
- **Target:** net collection 94% → 98%; denials down.
- **Current:** **92%** and denials **up** — moving the wrong way. **~$6M annualized cash drag.**
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) net collections ≥ 95%; (2) denial rate down 200bps; (3) payor-specific denial playbooks live — now supportable with payor analytics under the new CFO.
- **Risk:** Function out-scaled by multi-payor complexity; perf plan recommended.

## Workstream 3 — Clinician retention  ·  Owner: VP Clinical (Ortiz) + COO (Nair)  ·  🔴
- **Target:** attrition 24% → 15%. **Current: 28%** — worse. Driving **locum/agency costs up ~$4M.**
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) attrition < 22%; (2) a funded retention + comp plan; (3) clinician NPS up.
- **Risk:** Function under-resourced; **funding now unblocked with the CFO in seat** — the retention budget can finally be approved.

## Workstream 4 — Payor mix / rate improvement  ·  Owner: CFO (Okonkwo)  ·  🟢 unblocked
- **Target:** +300bps blended rate via payor-mix shift and contract renegotiation.
- **Current:** **unblocked** — Naomi Okonkwo joined as CFO 2026-05-19 and now owns payor analytics and contracting. The ~$18M of bridge value tied to this workstream finally has an owner.
- **What changed (EV impact):** Filling the CFO seat removes the single biggest overhang on the business; the operating team estimates **~+$10M EV impact** from a step-change finance function (13-week cash model rebuilt, lender relationship stabilized).
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) payor analytics stood up; (2) two contracts renegotiated; (3) durable monthly close + rolling forecast.

## Workstream 5 — MSO / central back-office  ·  Owner: COO (Nair)  ·  🟢
- **Target:** central scheduling, credentialing, billing, and HR shared services.
- **Current:** **live and on track** — built and run by the COO. The steady-state engine of the deal.
- **3–6 month indicators of success:** (1) all 52 clinics on central scheduling; (2) back-office cost-per-clinic down 10%.

## Workstream 6 — EBITDA bridge  ·  Owner: CFO (Okonkwo)  ·  🟡 stabilizing
- **Target:** $14M → $24M. **Current: $11M.** Mix of stalled growth, weak collections, and locum costs — now stabilizing as the CFO seat is filled and commercial recovers.
