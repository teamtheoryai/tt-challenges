# Organizational Due Diligence — Project Cedar (Cascade Care Group)

**Confidential — prepared for the DAW CAPITAL Investment Committee ahead of close.**

## 1. Purpose & method

Assesses the leadership of Cascade Care Group ("Cascade," "the Company") and sets the **talent baseline at deal close**. Each executive was scored against the DAW target scorecard for their role (1–5 scale), via structured interview, reference calls, and review of the prior owner's records. Scores in §4 become the **Interview Performance baseline** (`basis: org-DD-at-close`) for the executives carried into the talent review.

## 2. Company & thesis context

Cascade is a Portland-based network of 48 outpatient clinics (integrated behavioral health + primary care) across OR, WA, ID, and NV (~$120M revenue, ~$14M EBITDA, ~1,900 FTE incl. ~340 clinicians). DAW's thesis is a **de-novo growth + operations-improvement play**: grow to 80 clinics, lift net collection rate from 94% toward 98%, cut clinician attrition, build a central MSO, and expand EBITDA to ~$24M.

The thesis is **execution-heavy and people-dependent**: it needs a CEO who can drive disciplined multi-site growth, a CFO who can build payor analytics and an MSO finance engine, a COO who can standardize clinic operations, and a commercial leader who can build a repeatable de-novo growth and patient-acquisition engine. Diligence rates **management as the single biggest risk to the thesis** — see §6.

## 4. Executive assessments & baseline scores

Scale: 1 = significant concern · 3 = meets · 5 = exceptional. RYG: 1–2 red, 3 yellow, 4–5 green.

### 4.1 Hank Whitford — CEO (founder-era, retained)
- **Tenure at close:** 15 months (founder-era operator, retained).
- **Baseline score: 3.6 (yellow).** Likable, clinically credible, strong local relationships. **Question mark on whether he can run a disciplined 80-clinic growth machine** — he is a builder, not yet a scaler.
- **Direct cost:** $720,000 total target comp.
- **Reference signal:** "Hank is the heart of Cascade. Whether he's the CEO for the next phase is a fair question." (former board member).
- **Succession at close:** COO (Nair) is the obvious successor.
- **Risk at entry:** Strategy discipline; tendency to chase new sites over fixing existing-clinic economics. **CEO fit for the next phase is the open question carried into the hold.**

### 4.2 CFO — J. Marsh (flagged retention risk)
- **Tenure at close:** 11 months.
- **Baseline score: 3.2 (yellow).** Adequate controller-level finance; **not the payor-analytics / MSO-finance profile the thesis needs.** Diligence flags him as a likely early departure.
- **Direct cost:** $480,000.
- **Succession at close:** **None.** A controller (J. Liu) sits beneath.
- **Risk at entry:** **CFO is the wrong profile for the thesis and a flight risk** — plan for a near-term replacement.

### 4.3 Priya Nair — COO
- **Tenure at close:** 6 months (recruited by prior owner just before sale).
- **Baseline score: 3.7 (green).** The strongest executive. Disciplined multi-site operator from a larger care platform; the assessor's clear CEO-successor.
- **Direct cost:** $480,000.
- **Reference signal:** "If you only keep one person here, keep Priya." (reference).
- **Succession at close:** A regional director is a developing successor.
- **Risk at entry:** **Will she stay if Hank stays?** She joined expecting a faster path to CEO. **Retention risk flagged at entry.**

### 4.4 CMO (Chief Marketing / Commercial Officer) — open seat at close
- **Status at close:** **Vacant.** The prior owner ran growth informally through the founder-CEO; there is no commercial leader who owns de-novo site selection, patient acquisition, and the growth pipeline.
- **Plan:** Recruit an external CMO to own the **commercial engine** the growth thesis depends on. (Seat is filled by Trevor Hale in 2025 — see interview notes.)
- **Risk at entry:** **The growth thesis (48 → 80 clinics) has no dedicated owner at close.** This is a day-1 hiring priority.

### 4.5 W. Tran — VP RCM
- **Tenure at close:** 0 months (joins at close).
- **Baseline score: 3.5 (yellow).** Solid RCM background; **unproven at this scale and across this many payors/states.**
- **Direct cost:** $300,000.
- **Succession at close:** None.
- **Risk at entry:** Net collection rate (94%) and denials are below where the thesis needs them; this seat must deliver.

### 4.6 Dr. L. Ortiz — VP Clinical (clinician retention & quality)
- **Tenure at close:** 8 months.
- **Baseline score: 3.4 (yellow).** Respected clinical leader; the clinician-retention function is small and underbuilt for a sector-wide attrition crisis.
- **Direct cost:** $270,000.
- **Risk at entry:** Clinician attrition is the company's most dangerous operating metric and the function is under-resourced.

## 6. Key talent risks at entry (for the IC)

1. **CEO fit for the next phase** — a builder being asked to become a disciplined scaler.
2. **CFO is the wrong profile and a flight risk** — plan a replacement; the thesis needs payor analytics + MSO finance.
3. **COO retention** — the best executive may leave if the CEO transition stalls.
4. **No commercial owner at close** — the de-novo growth engine needs a dedicated CMO hire.
5. **Clinician attrition** — the metric most able to erode value, with an under-resourced clinical function.

## 7. Recommendation

Proceed to close **with a management-change reserve.** Talent is the **primary risk** to this thesis, not an enabler. Day-1 priorities: line up a CFO replacement, hire a commercial CMO, lock in the COO with a clear path, and resource clinician retention. Re-assess the full team at 18 months — **and be prepared to make a CEO change if the growth discipline does not appear.**
