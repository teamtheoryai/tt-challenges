# DAW CAPITAL — Portfolio-Wide Talent Thesis & Assessment Philosophy

**Confidential — fund-level talent doctrine. The companion to the shared `methodology/` brain.**

## 1. The core belief

In the lower middle market, **talent is the asset.** The businesses DAW buys are durable but under-led; the difference between a 2x and a 4x is almost always the quality, fit, and retention of the executive team. We therefore treat leadership assessment as a **primary, continuous diligence question** — underwritten at entry, re-tested every quarter, and reported to the IC and boards with the same rigor as financials.

## 2. One methodology, every portco

Every executive at PC1, PC2, and PC3 is scored against the **same** assessment scorecard so the fund can compare leadership risk and EV exposure on a like-for-like basis. The shared brain lives in `methodology/`:

- `scorecard.md` — the assessment dimensions and 1–5 scale (mapped to Red / Yellow / Green).
- `definitions.md` — what each metric means (RYG, 9-box, succession readiness, signal).
- `ev-impact-model.md` — how people-linked EV impact and erosion are estimated.
- `scoring-rules.md` — evidence rules and how to weight contradicting sources.

**Rule of the house:** no score without evidence. Every rating traces to a quote, a document, a location, and a speaker. The portfolio talent-review platform exists to keep that picture live as new documents arrive.

## 3. The talent baseline

The anchor for every later judgment is the **org due-diligence assessment at deal close** (`basis: org-DD-at-close`). That baseline establishes each executive's score at entry; every subsequent board deck, 360, or VCP review is read as *current vs. baseline*. Drift in either direction is the signal we act on.

## 4. What we assess

For each executive we maintain:
- **Score vs. baseline** against the shared scorecard (1–5, RYG).
- **9-box** position (performance × potential).
- **Tracking on VCP** — are they delivering the workstream they own?
- **Succession readiness** — who backs them up, and how ready.
- **People-linked EV at risk** — the value exposed if this leader underperforms or leaves.
- **Recommended action** — concrete, time-bound, owned.

## 5. Succession philosophy

Because our deal flow *is* succession, we hold a high standard for it inside the portfolio too:

1. **Every key seat has a named successor and a readiness rating.** A seat with no bench is a flagged risk, not a footnote.
2. **De-single-thread the irreplaceable.** Where a workstream depends on one person (a technical platform, a lender relationship), we build a deputy and document the relationships — *before* we need to.
3. **Founder transitions are planned, not triggered.** A retiring founder-CEO is handed off on a schedule, with the customer and institutional relationships transferred deliberately.
4. **Promote the proven operator.** Our best succession outcomes are internal operators who have already earned the team's trust.

## 6. The three portfolio talent pictures (FY26)

- **PC1 Vantage — strong core, concentrated risk.** CEO/COO/CTO track the thesis. The two largest exposures are **CTO flight risk** (~$26M of EV single-threaded on the cyber platform) and **CFO key-person risk** (no finance bench). Largest performance gap: the **CRO** on recurring-revenue conversion. *Action: retain and de-single-thread the CTO; build the finance bench; gate the CRO.*
- **PC2 Cascade — underperforming the thesis (ESCALATE).** Leadership is below plan as diligence warned. The recoverable path runs through **COO Priya Nair** — retain/promote her, protect the **newly filled CFO** seat (Naomi Okonkwo) and rebuild the finance bench, and resource clinician retention. **Losing the COO is the catastrophic scenario.** *Action: stabilize leadership now; the biggest swing factor in the fund.*
- **PC3 Ridgeline — healthy, succession-defined.** A strong, stable team delivering the plan with no executive below the bar. The defining task is the **orderly retirement of founder-CEO Hank Dreyer (~18 months) into COO Tom Whitman** — the best succession answer in the portfolio. *Action: execute the handoff cleanly; backfill the COO.*

## 7. How talent flows into the fund's numbers

People-linked EV at risk rolls up from the executive level to the portco summary to the fund view (see `portfolio-construction-and-ev`). A red executive is not just an HR issue — it is a quantified line in the value bridge, owned by a board member, with a named action and a date. That is the discipline DAW sells to its LPs: **the thing that actually drives returns is the thing we measure most carefully.**
