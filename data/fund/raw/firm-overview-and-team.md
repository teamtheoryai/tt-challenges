## 2. What "DAW" stands for

DAW is named for its three founders — **D**eline, **A**bara, and **W**inters — who built the firm around a shared conviction: in the lower middle market, **returns are made by the people running the business**, so the firm should be built around finding, backing, and developing leaders, not just sourcing assets.

## 5. LP base

Fund II's ~$450M is committed by a deliberately concentrated, long-duration LP base:

- **Family offices (~40%)** — including several former operators and SME owners who value the succession thesis.
- **Endowments & foundations (~25%)**.
- **Funds-of-funds & institutional PE allocators (~20%)**.
- **Insurance & pension capital (~10%)**.
- **GP commitment & firm employees (~5%)** — the team is a meaningful investor alongside LPs.

No single LP represents more than ~12% of Fund II commitments.

## 6. Governance & cadence

- **Investment Committee** (the three Managing Partners + lead Principal) approves all acquisitions, follow-ons, and exits.
- **Quarterly portfolio reviews** cover financial performance *and* a standing **leadership / talent risk** review against the shared methodology.
- **Valuation Committee** marks the book quarterly against the entry → current → target EV bridge.
- **Annual LP meeting** each Q1; quarterly LP letters.

## 7. Why LPs back DAW

A differentiated, **succession-driven** sourcing channel in an underserved part of the market; a repeatable, **people-first** value-creation model run as one system across the portfolio; an experienced operating bench led by the founders themselves; and an unusually transparent, evidence-backed way of reporting the thing that actually drives returns — **the quality and risk of portfolio leadership.**
