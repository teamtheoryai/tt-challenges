# DAW CAPITAL — Fund Strategy & Investment Thesis (Fund II)

**Confidential — for DAW CAPITAL Limited Partners.**

## 1. Who we are

DAW CAPITAL is a **lower-middle-market private equity firm** that buys control of established, founder- or family-owned businesses in the U.S. Mountain West and acquires them through leadership succession. We back proven operators into companies whose founders are ready for their next chapter, and we create value primarily through **people and operating discipline** rather than financial engineering.

We are, in spirit, a **talent-driven, succession-focused fund**: most of our pipeline comes from owners aging out of businesses they built, and our edge is the quality of the leadership team we install and develop after close. We do not chase trophy assets or auctions — we win on certainty of close, respect for legacy, and a credible plan for the people who will run the business next.

## 3. Why this thesis works

Thousands of operationally sound U.S. SMEs face a leadership cliff as their founders retire, with no institutional successor in place. These businesses are profitable and durable but **under-led and under-systematized**. The opportunity is not to fix a broken business — it is to put **better leadership and better operating cadence** behind a good one.

Our three value levers, in order of importance:

1. **Leadership succession & upgrade.** We install or develop a CEO and executive team capable of running the *future-state* business, not just the one we bought. This is where most of our EV is made or lost.
2. **Operating system.** We bring a consistent operating cadence — board rhythm, KPI discipline, value-creation planning, and a shared talent methodology — across every portfolio company.
3. **Buy-and-build & margin.** Where the market is fragmented, we add density and integrate tuck-ins; everywhere, we drive recurring-revenue mix and margin.

## 4. The DAW operating model

- **Observe before we act.** Post-close, we spend the first 100 days understanding culture, customers, and the team before making changes. Stable, well-run companies whose only real gap is succession get continuity, not disruption.
- **Legacy-preserving transitions.** Founders frequently roll equity and stay engaged as chairs or advisors. Trust between the outgoing owner and the incoming CEO is a deal term, not an afterthought.
- **One methodology, every portco.** Every executive across every portfolio company is assessed against the *same* talent methodology (see the shared `methodology/` brain), so the fund can compare leadership risk and EV exposure on a like-for-like basis.
- **Talent as a continuous diligence question.** We treat leadership assessment as a living process that updates as new evidence arrives — board decks, 360s, VCP reviews — not a one-time pre-close exercise.

## 5. How we measure ourselves

Every investment is underwritten to an **entry → current → target EV** value bridge, with named workstreams, owners, and 3–6 month indicators of success. People-linked EV at risk is tracked explicitly: where a workstream depends on a single leader, that concentration is named and managed. Returns are reported against the bridge, and **leadership risk is a standing board metric**, not a soft topic.

## 6. Responsible ownership

DAW invests for durable, real-economy returns: preserving jobs and institutional knowledge through succession, modernizing legacy operations, and holding our companies to clear, simple performance and governance standards rather than abstract ESG checklists. Good ownership and good returns are, for us, the same discipline.

## 7. The bottom line

DAW CAPITAL turns the retirement of America's SME founders into a repeatable, **people-first** private equity strategy. We buy good businesses at a moment of leadership transition, put exceptional and well-supported leaders behind them, and run one disciplined operating and talent system across the portfolio. **Talent is the thesis.**
