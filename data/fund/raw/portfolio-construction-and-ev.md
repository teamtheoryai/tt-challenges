## 3. Sector & risk spread

- **By target exit EV:** Transportation/logistics (PC3) ~38%, IT services (PC1) ~33%, Healthcare (PC2) ~28%.
- **Concentration:** No single platform is more than ~40% of projected gross value; PC3 is the largest position by EV.
- **Vintage clustering:** All three sit in a 2023–24 entry window, so the fund's realization window is similarly clustered (target exits ~2028–2030).

## 4. People-linked EV at risk (portfolio view)

DAW underwrites talent as a value driver, so EV exposure is tracked **per leader and per workstream**, not just per company.

| Company | People-linked EV at risk | Primary driver |
|---|---|---|
| PC1 Vantage | ~$26M (single largest exposure) | CTO single-threaded on the cyber/MDR platform; flight risk |
| PC2 Cascade | ~$31M | CEO missing VCP gates; CFO seat vacant; stalled commercial engine |
| PC3 Ridgeline | low / managed | Orderly founder-CEO succession in motion; deep bench |

**Portfolio headline:** the two largest pools of people-linked EV risk are **PC2 (broad leadership underperformance — escalate)** and **PC1 (concentration on a single technical leader — retain and de-single-thread).** PC3 is the model: a strong, stable team where the defining task is an *orderly, well-planned* CEO succession rather than a fix.

## 5. Capital deployed & dry powder

- **Invested (equity):** ~$285M across the three platforms.
- **Reserves:** ~$95M held for tuck-ins (notably PC1 buy-and-build) and follow-on support (PC2 turnaround).
- **Uncalled / available:** ~$70M of Fund II commitments remain for a potential fourth platform.

## 6. What the Valuation Committee is watching

1. **PC2 turnaround** — the single biggest swing factor in the fund mark; gated on the CFO hire, COO retention, and clinician attrition.
2. **PC1 mix** — EBITDA quality improves only when recurring-revenue conversion turns; CTO retention protects the cyber upside.
3. **PC3 succession** — protecting a healthy mark through a clean founder-CEO handoff and customer-relationship transfer.
