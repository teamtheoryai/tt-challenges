# The company and the mission — the short version

*Read this first. Everything else in this folder hangs off it.*

## What we believe

In private equity, the highest-leverage decisions aren't financial — they're **people decisions**. Who runs the company decides whether the plan happens. And yet the way funds make those decisions is the least instrumented part of the firm: a search produces hundreds of pages of interviews, references, assessments, and scorecards… that end up scattered in inboxes and drives, unreadable at the moment of decision and forgotten by the next search.

We build the fix: **a second brain for the fund's people decisions.** Every document from every search and every portfolio company becomes memory the fund can converse with, and that memory produces new artifacts — profiles, comparisons, briefs, reviews — that a partner can stand behind in the room where the decision gets made.

## The vision, in one sentence

> Every people decision a fund makes, made on evidence it can interrogate — and every decision making the next one smarter.

## What that means for anything you build

Three consequences follow from the mission, and they show up in everything we ship:

1. **Evidence is the product.** Our users defend conclusions to skeptical partners at investment committees. An output they can't trace is an output they can't use — no matter how good it looks.
2. **Memory compounds.** The system's value comes from what it keeps. Anything it generates should flow back in, not evaporate at the end of a session.
3. **Trust is structural.** Our customers handle sensitive people-data across companies that must never see each other's records. Isolation and honesty ("I don't know") are features, not constraints.

## Who we are commercially (the one-paragraph version)

We sell to lower-middle-market and mid-market PE funds — the fund's talent function is our buyer, the whole deal team is the audience for its outputs, and the expansion motion runs downward into the portfolio companies themselves. The fictional fund in this repo, **DAW Capital**, is a faithful stand-in for the customer we serve (see [04-accounts.md](04-accounts.md)).

*Everything in this folder is fictional or fictionalized — no real client names or data — but the mission, the user, and the jobs are true to what we build.*
