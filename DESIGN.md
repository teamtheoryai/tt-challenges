# DESIGN — brand & product guidance

*One page, directional, non-binding. If your taste beats ours, use yours — and say why in DECISIONS.md. What we're actually grading is whether the product feels like one coherent thing built for a specific person.*

## Who it has to feel right for

A fund talent partner and the deal partners they answer to. People who read board packs for a living, distrust dashboards that perform confidence, and will quote your product's output **in an investment-committee meeting**. The register is *calm, evidentiary, expensive* — a well-typeset memo, not a SaaS carnival.

## Product principles (these are the brand)

1. **Every number can be interrogated.** Score, claim, or summary — the evidence behind it is one interaction away, never a dead end.
2. **Status is always visible.** Ingesting, processing, failed, ready — the user never wonders whether the system is working.
3. **The brain never bluffs.** Uncited prose reads differently than cited prose, gaps are stated, and "I don't know" is styled as a first-class answer, not an error.
4. **Quiet surfaces, loud evidence.** Chrome recedes; sources, provenance, and flags are what earn visual weight.

## Starter visual language (optional)

| Token | Suggestion |
|---|---|
| Surface | warm paper `#FAF8F3`; cards `#FFFFFF`, 1px borders `#E3E0D8` |
| Ink | near-black `#1D2129`; muted `#6B7280` |
| Accent | one only — patina green `#2F6F4F` (actions, active states) |
| Signal | amber for attention, `#B3402F` for failure — sparingly; a flag that's always red is wallpaper |
| Type | serif for headings/documents (Georgia or similar), system sans for UI; generated documents should read like *documents*, not chat bubbles |
| Feel | generous whitespace, real tables, no gradients, no glassmorphism, no emoji in product surfaces |

## Anti-patterns (things that read as "didn't get it")

- Chat-app cosplay: the generated document rendered as a giant chat bubble.
- Confidence theater: green checkmarks and 98%-scores with nothing behind them.
- A dashboard of vanity counters ("42 documents!") that answers no user question.
- Ten accent colors doing the job of one.
